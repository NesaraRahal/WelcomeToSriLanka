Explore Sri Lanka

Visit Sri Lanka Now

Sri Lanka’s ancient kingdoms date back over 2,500 years, giving its wildlife, waterfalls, ruins and reams of tea plantations an aura of enlightenment. As an island nation, the country benefits from a wonderful feeling of remoteness, making it easy to lose yourself amidst the tropical rainforests, immaculate white-sand beaches and age-old towns. The southern Hill Country is home to Ella, with its rumbling Ravana Falls and the 1,300-metre-high cliff or Ella’s Rock - reachable via a scenic two-hour trek along a tea-house-laden railway track. Some of Sri Lanka’s best beaches can be found in Tangalla, Kalpitiya and Hikkaduwa, with some offering water sports, dolphin and whale watching, and snorkeling. For safari adventures, the country boasts a wealth of stunning national parks. Yala is the best spot for leopards, while Udawalawe and Wasgamuwa are home to parades of wild elephants. If you’re in search of ancient ruins, head for the sites of Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa to discover architectural gems such as huge dagobas (dome-shaped structures containing relics). Sri Lanka offers a wide range of beach hotels, and mountain top villas surrounded by tea plantations. There’s also a variety of apartments, B&Bs and independent homestays available.

Places to visit in Sri Lanka

Sigiriya

Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress in Sri Lanka, renowned for its historical and architectural significance. Rising abruptly from the central plains, it's crowned by a palace complex built during the 5th century.Its sheer vertical walls, adorned with colorful frescoes and a massive stone lion's paw, captivate visitors. Sigiriya's ingenious water gardens and intricate irrigation system reflect advanced engineering for its time. Often termed the "Eighth Wonder of the World," Sigiriya showcases the fusion of artistry and innovation from the ancient world, offering a glimpse into the island's rich cultural heritage and the brilliance of its past civilizations.

Kandy

Kandy, nestled amidst Sri Lanka's lush hills, holds significant cultural and historical importance. It's renowned for the Temple of the Tooth Relic, housing a sacred tooth of Buddha, making it a revered pilgrimage site for Buddhists. The city's picturesque lake, Kandy Lake, adds to its charm. Beyond its religious significance, Kandy boasts rich traditions showcased in its vibrant festivals, particularly the Esala Perahera, a grand procession featuring splendidly adorned elephants, dancers, and drummers parading through the streets. The Royal Botanical Gardens in nearby Peradeniya are a botanical marvel, showcasing diverse flora. With its blend of spirituality, natural beauty, and cultural richness, Kandy stands as a captivating destination in Sri Lanka.

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura stands as an ancient city in Sri Lanka, holding immense historical and cultural significance. It was the country's first capital and a thriving center of Buddhist civilization for over a millennium. The city's vast complex of ruins showcases magnificent stupas, monasteries, palaces, and ancient irrigation systems. The sacred Bodhi Tree, believed to have grown from a cutting of the original tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment, resides in Anuradhapura, making it a revered pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. The city reflects the advanced engineering and architectural prowess of its time, evident in the creation of enormous stupas like Ruwanwelisaya and Abhayagiriya, as well as the intricate irrigation networks such as the Tissa Wewa reservoir. Today, Anuradhapura stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, drawing visitors and historians alike to explore its archaeological marvels and gain insight into the rich cultural heritage of Sri Lanka.

Udawalawe

Udawalawe National Park in Sri Lanka is a renowned wildlife sanctuary, celebrated for its diverse biodiversity and captivating landscapes. Nestled in the country's southern region, it's a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. The park is particularly famous for its large population of Asian elephants, offering visitors a unique opportunity to witness these majestic creatures in their natural habitat. The expansive grasslands, shimmering reservoir, and dense forests create an ideal environment for a variety of species, including buffalo, deer, and an array of birdlife. Safari adventures through Udawalawe promise an immersive experience, blending nature's beauty with wildlife encounters.

Arugambay

Arugam Bay, nestled on Sri Lanka's eastern coast, is a paradise for surfers and beach enthusiasts. Renowned for its consistent waves and laid-back atmosphere, it offers a haven for both beginners and experienced surfers. Beyond surfing, Arugam Bay captivates with its pristine beaches, stunning sunsets, and a relaxed, bohemian vibe. The surrounding nature reserves and lagoons add to its charm, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a blend of adventure and tranquility on Sri Lanka's coastline.

Nuwara-Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, often dubbed as Sri Lanka's "Little England," is a picturesque hill station nestled in the island's central highlands. Known for its temperate climate, lush tea plantations, and colonial-era architecture, it offers a refreshing escape from the tropical heat. The region's rolling hills are adorned with neatly trimmed tea bushes, while its cool air and scenic landscapes make it a favored destination for nature lovers and tea enthusiasts. Beyond tea, Nuwara Eliya boasts serene lakes, waterfalls, and opportunities for hiking, showcasing its natural beauty and earning its place as a charming retreat in Sri Lanka's scenic heartland.

Culture in Sri Lanka

Religious diversity

Sri Lanka's religious landscape is characterized by a harmonious coexistence of multiple faiths. The majority of the population adheres to Buddhism, with ancient temples and the revered Temple of the Tooth in Kandy embodying the nation's Buddhist heritage. In addition to Buddhism, sizable Hindu, Muslim, and Christian communities thrive, enhancing the country's cultural vibrancy. Hinduism is practiced by a significant portion, with colorful festivals and temples dotting the landscape. Islam, embraced by a substantial Muslim population, contributes its own distinctive traditions, while Christianity, introduced during the colonial era, has left an indelible mark on Sri Lanka's religious mosaic. This religious diversity fosters a unique cultural identity, where various religious practices and celebrations coalesce to shape the social fabric of the nation. The cohabitation of these faiths reflects the tolerance and mutual respect that define Sri Lanka's pluralistic society.

Cultural festivals

Sri Lanka's endemic cultural festivals embody the nation's rich heritage. Vesak, a Buddhist celebration, illuminates the country with lanterns and processions, commemorating significant events in Buddha's life. The grand Esala Perahera in Kandy revolves around the veneration of the Sacred Tooth Relic, featuring traditional dances and adorned elephants. Kandy Perahera extends this cultural display with captivating processions. The Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil Festival in Jaffna is a prominent Hindu celebration, marked by elaborate rituals and vibrant processions. Additionally, the Madhu Church Feast draws devotees of various backgrounds to the Shrine of Our Lady of Madhu for Catholic pilgrimage and festivities. These festivals collectively illustrate Sri Lanka's diverse cultural fabric, fostering unity and shared joy among its people.

Traditional Arts

Sri Lanka's traditional arts and crafts showcase a vibrant cultural tapestry. In the realm of dance and music, the Kandyan dance stands out with its elaborate costumes and rhythmic movements, representing a classical form deeply rooted in the island's history. Traditional music encompasses diverse expressions, from mesmerizing drum ensembles to the melodic tunes of flutes and various folk forms, reflecting the multifaceted nature of Sri Lankan musical traditions.

Traditional Crafts

The country's reputation for craftsmanship is exemplified through intricate wood carving, batik art, and traditional mask making. These handicrafts not only display remarkable skill but also carry profound cultural and religious significance. Wood carving often adorns temples with intricate designs, while batik art reflects vibrant colors and patterns. Traditional mask making, with its ritualistic roots, contributes to the island's rich cultural heritage, embodying a synthesis of artistic expression and spiritual symbolism in Sri Lanka's traditional arts and crafts.

Ayurveda

Sri Lanka boasts a rich tradition of Ayurveda, a time-honored system of traditional medicine with roots dating back centuries. Ayurveda, meaning "science of life," is deeply ingrained in the country's cultural and healthcare practices. Sri Lanka's commitment to Ayurveda is evident in the widespread adoption of its treatments and wellness programs. Many resorts across the island specialize in offering Ayurvedictherapies, leveraging natural ingredients and holistic approaches to promote health and balance. Ayurvedic treatments encompass a range of practices, including herbal remedies, dietary adjustments, and rejuvenating therapies tailored to individual needs. Visitors to Sri Lanka can immerse themselves in Ayurvedic wellness experiences, enjoying the benefits of a system that harmonizes physical, mental, and spiritual well-being, contributing to the enduring legacy of traditional medicine in the country.

Sri Lankan Cuisine

Sri Lankan cuisine is a culinary delight, defined by its staple of rice and curry. Aromatic spices and flavors infuse these dishes, creating a rich tapestry of taste. Coconut, a culinary cornerstone, imparts a distinctive and delicious quality to many Sri Lankan recipes. Beyond rice and curry, hoppers, bowl-shaped pancakes with acrispy edge, and roti, a versatile flatbread, stand out as popular snacks and breakfast items. These culinary staples reflect the diverse and flavorful essence of Sri Lankan gastronomy, where the artful use of spices and the prominence of coconut contribute to a unique and satisfying dining experience.
